I'm a designer, developer and mum of two living in Sheffield, UK. I take a human-centered approach to design and development in order to create useful and enjoyable experiences for people.

I was born and raised in Texas. I moved to the UK after I graduated from university in 2010. I majored in Anthropology, the study of people and cultures. I have always been fascinated by the way people think and how that changes across time and space. I have a background in admin which really helped to develop my problem-solving skills. In every job I have had I always try to design processes that are efficient and repeatable.

I'm studying user experience design through Google's UX Professional course and I'm a self taught web developer. I owe my success to the 100 Days of Code challenge which took me from beginner coder to freelance web designer/developer in 100 days. Learning UX design has really helped to level up my skills. I love creating personas and user stories and the problem-solving aspect of prototyping. I am looking for a challenging role with a fun work environment where I can design products that make a big impact on people.

You can read about why I learned to code in my blog post: You Can't Be What You Can't See.
