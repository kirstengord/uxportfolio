artist portfolio redesign

Allowing the artwork to shine.

Chuck Roach is a contemporary western and portrait artist in West Texas. His portfolio lacked a consistent design and was confusing to navigate. I created a clean, minimalist, and completely responsive design that allows the artwork to take center stage.

my role
Responsible for creating a new logo, website redesign and updating site on Weebly

timeline
1 week - March 2021

project type
freelance

Before

The wood pattern on the homepage distracted the user from the artwork and the font and color of the text made it difficult to read. There were no links to the retail locations.

After

I chose a monotone colour palette to show off the artwork and kept the design consistent throughout the website. I created a new page with links to all of Chuck's retail locations making it easy for the user to get information and find directions to the different galleries.

Before

Because all of the portfolio images had been sized the same, many of the images were cut off. The images were not organized into categories making it difficult for the user to find certain images and get a feel for the different types of work that Chuck does.

After

I changed the sizing of the images so that each image was fully visible. I also catagorized the images by genre making them easier to navigate.

Before

The all-caps serif font on this page was difficult to read.

After

I changed the font to open-sans to make it easier to read.

Before

Chuck's contact page was not user friendly with no links to his social media or email.

After

I added buttons with links to call, email, and navigate to his Facebook page where he does most of his business.

Logo

Chuck did not have a logo. I decided to use his initials to make a logo in the style of a cattle brand to fit the western theme in his artwork.
