DAILY UI

100 Days of UI Challenges

OVERVIEW

The Challenge

Daily UI is a design challenge created to inspire and challenge designers around the world. I signed up for this challenge through the Daily UI website. I use these challenges as a brain break from my professional work. It is nice to stop sometimes and work on something that is purely creative and completely up to me how to design.

The Goal

My goal is to improve my visual design and layout skills and get more practice using Figma.

DAILY UI PROJECTS

Challenge 001 - Sign Up

This challenge allowed for a sign up form for any device and any product. I chose a music festival app that you would use to find lineups and create your own lineup. All of the colors were checked with a WCAG contrast checker and all icons are supplemented with text for accessibility. The mint color is bright but it is balanced with the use of black and white and a good amount of white space.

Challenge 002 - Card Payment

This is not my favourite design but...that's ok! I gave myself a time limit of 1 hour to do this challenge from start to finish. At the end of that hour, instead of telling myself, “I really don’t like this.” I choose to reflect on what I learned today.
✅ I discovered unDraw for free illustrations.
✅ I’ve figured out horizontal scrolling in Figma.
✅ I used Smart Animate for the first time (It’s awesome!)
✅ I figured out how to animate a progress indicator!

Challenge 003 - Landing Page

The 1 hour time limit I gave myself made this challenge really difficult! It took 15 min just to decide on what product to use. I just finshed reading The Midnight Library so I went with that. I chose the yellow circle behind the book too mirror the illuminated "i" in the book title and made the call to action buttons a high contrasting blue to make them stand out.
