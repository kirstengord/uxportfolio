texicana

Texicana is a concept food truck app created for the Google UX Design course. I was tasked with creating a visual menu app. Research showed that the coronavirus pandemic has changed customer attitudes to queues so I went one step further and created a visual menu and ordering app that helps to maximise social time.

My Role

I was responsible for the entire UX design process: Research, Wireframes, Protoypes, Visual Design, Testing

Timeline

6 weeks - April | May 2021

the problem

The pandemic has drasticaly changed consumer behavior. We are all much more likely to shop online and order takeaways from our phones. How do we encourage people to get out and enjoy themselves again?

the goal

Create an app that allows people to feel safe to socialise by automating the ordering process and reducing the need to queue.

my process

I began by conducting interviews to confirm assumptions about consumer behavior changes. I used this information to create a persona, user story, and storyboard which helped to keep the user front and center during the ideation phase. I researched competitors using ordering apps for ideas and pain points before creating a user flow and then paper and digital wireframes. After round 1 testing with the low fidelity prototype, I created mockups and a high fidelity prototype for a second round of testing.

persona

In order to better understand the user for this project, I create the persona of Georgina who became my travel companion on this design journey, helping me to better understand the project from her point of view and to create the best possible user experience.

Meet Georgina...

Education: University student, 1st year

Hometown: Huddersfield, Yorkshire

2. Define

Through research I confirmed my initial assumptions that people going to a food truck would benefit from a visual menu. Research also revealed that many people are worried about social distancing, but this was not the only factor. People expressed the desire to spend as much time as possible with friends and family that they are meeting with and would therefore prefer to order through an app rather than wait in a queue.

The Problem

The coronavirus pandemic has caused a shift in user attitudes to waiting in a queue. People want an easy way to order and pay for their food that minimises wait times and leaves more time to visit with friends and family.

3. Ideate

During the ideation phase, I researched direct and indirect competitors to see what they were doing well and what I could improve on. I looked at two food halls in Sheffield that currently use apps for ordering to generate ideas for user flows and layouts. I created "How might we...?" questions to explore possible problems and solutions. After thoughtful research, I ideated paper and digital wireframes.

Competitive Analysis

Kommune

The Kommune app overall is very good. It is slightly confusing the first time you open it. The button to choose how to order is at the bottom of the page and there are several other links that are not relate to ordering. This initial flow to get to the menus could be improved.

Cutlery Works

The Cutlery Works app is a web app so it can be opened on any phone or device with a web browser. This is not as convenient as a mobile app though. The app is slightly confusing because it also has other food vendors that are not located at Cutlery Works although when you are at Cutlery Works and have location services on it puts Cutlery Works vendors at the top of the list. I like that this app saves previous orders for future use.

How might we...?

How might we improve the experience of ordering through an app?

  • It could be benificial to have a mobile app as well as a web app so that the customer could choose to download it or use their browser.
  • Don't add any unnecessary information to the start screen.

How might we make the app simple to use?

  • After signing in, have the app go straight to the menu screen
  • Create a help section

How might we encourage people to use the app?

  • Loyalty program
  • Discount the first time you use the app

How might we create a visual menu?

  • Use large photos on the initial menu screen as well as the item screen.
  • Have a carousel of menu images at the top of the menu.

Affinity Map - Study #1

Insights - Study #1

1

Payment Confirmation

Users expect to see a confirmation page before their payment is taken in order to confirm amount and payment method. On the cart page change the "Pay" button to "Confirm Order" and add a payment confirmation page.

2

Payment Update

Users want an easier way to update payment information. On the payment confirmation page add an option to update payment method.

3

Cash Payments

Users want the option to pay with cash. On the payment confirmation page add the option to pay with cash at the food truck window.

Insights - Study #2

1

Logo Colors

All three participants agreed that the logo was very bright. Tone down the colors of the logo.

2

Menu Browsing

Users want an easier way to view menu sections. Add a horizontal scroll menu section bar at the top of the menu screen.

What I Learned

Mistake 1

I didn't do enough research. I should have done a survey to back up the findings from the interviews. This would have made my design decisions easier to explain and defend to stakeholders. Research is the foundation of the design. Going forward I will always spend more time on the research phase.

Mistake 2

Texicana is a concept business. If I had chosen a real food truck then I would have been able to do even more user research by looking at reviews. It's best to solve real problems.

Mistake 3

I didnt pay enough attention to the research when I started wireframing. I noted the need for cash payments in my user journey, but I didn't have that option in my first prototype. Going forward I will keep my research front and center while I'm designing.

All three of these mistakes stem from not understanding the Design Thinking process. The course teaches the process step by step as you complete this first project. By the end, all the pieces fell into place and I could see where I had gone wrong. I look forward to improving in future projects.

By the prototyping stage of this project I knew that UX design was the path for me. I absolutely love it. I learned how to complete the entire UX design process from empathizing with people to prototyping and testing. I loved creating Georgina's story and using it to inform my design. I especially enjoyed building the prototype.
