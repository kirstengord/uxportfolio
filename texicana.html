TEXICANA

Menu and Ordering App

Overview

My Role

This is a concept project for the Google UX Design course. I was responsible for the entire UX design process: Research, Wireframes, Protoypes, Visual Design, Testing

Timeline

4 weeks - May 2021

Tools Used

Pen and paper, Figma, Miro, Canva

The Problem

The coronavirus pandemic has caused a shift in customer attitudes to waiting in a queue. Customers want an easy way to order and pay for their food that minimises wait times and leaves more time to visit with friends and family.

The Solution

The Texicana app will allow customers visiting the food truck to order and pay for their food without having to queue. It will also have a visual menu so that customers have a better idea of what each menu item is.

My Process

1. Empathize

My prompt for this project was "Design a visual menu app for a food truck in your hometown". I began the empathize phase by thinking about why I would want to use a visual menu app. I love food and I really enjoying trying out different restaurants and food vendors. I often find myself looking around at what everyone else is eating to try to figure out what to order. For me, a visual menu woud be extremely helpful in deciding what to order. I needed to find out if other people felt the same way.

Hypothesis

People will benefit from a visual menu app when ordering from a food truck because it is easier to look at a picture than to try to visualize a menu item from text.

Interviews

Participants

3 female, 2 male, ages 19-64
All participants eat at a food truck or food stall at least twice a month.

Format

3 interviews were conducted in person, 1 over Google Meet and 1 through Facebook Messenger

Do you enjoy eating at food trucks or food stalls? Why or why not?

"Yeah I love it. Especially after being stuck at home for so long. It's nice to go out and do something 'normal'."

Has the coronavirus pandemic changed your attitude towards eating out?

"I'm normally not one to worry about germs but yeah, I do feel more aware of the people around me now. You always have in the back of your mind '2 meters, 2 meters'."

Can you think of a time when you were annoyed while ordering or eating at a food truck?

"I was at Peddler Market once and had to queue for 40 min. It was a nightmare. That would really annoy me now when all you want to do is spend time with the people you are out with. We've spent so long away from each other I think now you just want to make every second count."

Is there anything that would make ordering at a food truck easier for you?

"I always wish I could see everything on the menu. I see what other people have sometimes and I'm like 'I should have got that!'"

"I have a big group of friends and one of us always ends up being the one to wait in line and order for everyone else. Someone's order always gets messed up because there is too much to remember. So an easier way to do big group orders would be good."

Pain Points

1

Queues

Customers don't like long queues because it takes time away from friends and family. Some cutomers are also hesitant to wait near others outside of their social bubble.

2

Viewing Menu Items

Customers expressed the desire to be able to see pictures of menu items before they ordered. It is easier to decide from pictures than text.

3

Group Orders

Customers wanted an easier way to place big group orders. It is difficult to remember a large order.

2. Define

Through research I confirmed my initial assumptions that customers would benefit from a visual menu. Research also revealed that many people are worried about social distancing, but this was not the only factor. People expressed the desire to spend as much time as possible with friends and family that they are meeting with and therefore would prefer to order through an app rather than wait in a queue.

The Problem

The coronavirus pandemic has caused a shift in customer attitudes to waiting in a queue. Customers want an easy way to order and pay for their food that minimises wait times and leaves more time to visit with friends and family.

3. Ideate

During the ideation phase, I researched direct and indirect competitors to see what they were doing well and what I could improve on. I looked at two food halls in Sheffield that currently use apps for ordering to generate ideas for user flows and layouts. I created "How might we...?" questions to explore possible problems and solutions. After thoughtful research, I ideated user flows and layouts on paper wireframes.

Competitive Analysis

Kommune

The Kommune app overall is very good. It is slightly confusing the first time you open it. The button to choose how to order is at the bottom of the page and there are several other links that are not relate to ordering. This initial flow to get to the menus could be improved.

Cutlery Works

The Cutlery Works app is a web app so it can be opened on any phone or device with a web browser. This is not as convenient as a movile app though. The app is slightly confusing because it also has other food vendors that are not located at Cutlery Works although when you are at Cutlery Works and have location services on it puts Cutlery Works vendors at the top of the list. I like that this app saves previous orders for future use.

How might we...?

How might we improve the experience of ordering through an app?

  • It could be benificial to have a mobile app as well as a web app so that the customer could choose to download it or use their browser.
  • Don't add any unnecessary information to the start screen.

How might we make the app simple to use?

  • After signing in, have the app go straight to the menu screen
  • Create a help section

How might we encourage people to use the app?

  • Loyalty program
  • Discount the first time you use the app

How might we create a visual menu?

  • Use large photos on the initial menu screen as well as the item screen.
  • Have a carousel of menu images at the top of the menu.

5. Test

Study #1

I conducted an in-person moderated usability study with 5 participants (3 female, 2 male, ages 30-65) using the low fidelity prototype

Study #2

I conducted an in-person moderated usability study with 3 participants (2 female, 1 male, ages 36-42) using the high fidelity prototype

Affinity Map - Study #1

Insights - Study #1

1

Payment Confirmation

Customers expect to see a confirmation page before their payment is taken in order to confirm amount and payment method.

2

Payment Update

Customers want an easier way to update payment information.

3

Cash Payments

Customers want the option to pay with cash.

Insights - Study #2

1

Logo Colors

All three participants agreed that the logo was very bright. Tone down the colors of the logo.

2

Menu Browsing

Customers want an easier way to view menu sections. Add a horizontal scroll menu section bar at the top of the menu screen.
