talent hatched

Simplifying the recruitment process.

Talent Hatched is a startup based in San Antonio, TX currently participating in the 2021 Geekdom incubator. The Talent Hatched system aims to disrupt the current HR process by removing the need for paper resumes and cover letters. Talent Hatched brings back the human factor through onboarding and matches students with paid experiential learning opportunities.

overview

When I started on this project I was working for a company called Work In Industry Now (Wiin). Due to legal reasons the company was forced to dissolve and rebrand as Talent Hatched. The system consists of 4 user accounts: Applicants, Adminitrators, Employers, and a Superuser account. I have been involved in the research and planning of the entire system including conducting interviews, creating personas, user journeys, user flows, and designing the UI. This project is still under development and falls under a NDA. Unfortunately I can't share all of my hard work with you but I would like to share some of the challenges we have faced and how we have overcome them.

reshuffle, rethink, redefine

In May of 2021 I was tasked with creating a design prototype for the Q2 presentation in early July. I began designing for a backend program that used AI/machine learning to match potential skill sets with opportunities.

Three weeks before the Q2 presentation, the CTO and back-end developer, quit the incubator and left us with no code and no algorithm. The design up to that point relied on the assumption that machine learning would be involved in the matching process.

It was a disaster.

Did we give up? No way! Crisis often leads to opportunity. We put on our superwoman capes and figured it out.

We had a gruelling 3 hour product meeting where we rethought our strategy and redefined our MVP. Karen, our front-end developer, agreed to go full-stack. We left the meeting with a sharper vision and a better product.

I spent the next 3 weeks redesigning the prototype for the Applicant account while Sandra, the founder, spent countless hours practicing our pitch. Needless to say, we smashed it. The investors loved the pitch and one of them even offered to participate in our pilot.
